üîÑ System Workflow

Background: Current PA Process

Understanding the current prior authorization workflow helps illustrate where I-DocAssist provides value:

Current PA Process Flow:

  1. Patient reports symptoms to physician
  2. Physician pulls patient history from EHR System (Labs, Imaging, Therapy Records, Clinical Notes)
  3. Physician provides diagnosis to patient
  4. Clinical documentation is recorded in EHR
  5. Physician requests procedure (CPT/ICD-10 codes) from Revenue Cycle Team
  6. Revenue Cycle Team pulls history from EHR and pulls insurance docs from Insurance Document DB
  7. PA Request Package assembled: CPT/ICD-10 codes, Clinical Notes, Labs Reports, Imaging, Therapy Records, Insurance Docs
  8. Revenue Cycle Team submits PA package to Insurer
  9. Insurer makes decision based on policy requirements ‚Üí If denied, enters Appeal Loops

Problem: Manual documentation gathering and checklist verification is time-consuming and error-prone, leading to delays and appeals.

Our Solution: I-DocAssist Task Framing

I-DocAssist automates the requirement checking process in two stages:

Two-Stage Automation:

  • Stage 1 - Requirement Extraction & Checking:
    • Extract requirements from Insurance Docs
    • Check patient data against requirements using NLI
    • Identify which requirements are met/unmet
  • Stage 2 - Interactive Clarification:
    • Physician: Receives clinical evidence checklist (flagging unmet clinical prerequisites)
    • Revenue Cycle Team: Receives documentation checklist (flagging missing evidence or documentation gaps)

Interactive Workflow Example

Here's how I-DocAssist interacts with a physician during PA verification for cervical laminectomy surgery (CPT 63040):

Step 1: Physician Initiates Request

What happens:

  1. Physician states: "I am gonna do cervical laminectomy surgery (CPT 63040) to patient A"
  2. I-DocAssist makes two tool calls:
    • get_checklist(CPT) - Retrieves policy requirements for CPT 63040
    • get_ehr(patient A) - Fetches patient's EHR data
  3. Reasoning (NLI): Agent checks if each requirement is met based on available evidence

Step 2: Agent Identifies Gaps and Seeks Clarification

What happens:

  1. Physician's formal request: "I intend to perform cervical laminectomy (CPT 63040) for patient A. Please verify whether the patient meets the policy requirements and identify any missing documentation."
  2. Agent's response after checking:

    "For CPT 63040, the policy appears to require:

    1. At least 6 weeks of failed conservative treatment (documented)
    2. Advanced imaging (CT/MRI) supporting the indication

    I can't confirm eligibility yet based on the records I found. Could you confirm whether these exist, or point me to where they are documented?"

  3. Interactive clarification: The physician can now:
    • Confirm the evidence exists and provide location
    • Acknowledge the gap and schedule required documentation
    • Correct any misunderstanding by the agent
